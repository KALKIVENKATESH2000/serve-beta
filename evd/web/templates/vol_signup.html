eVidyaloka is a not-for-profit organization that focuses on transforming the educational landscape of rural India. Registered under the Trusts Act, the organization creates digital classrooms for children in remote villages, with the support of local communities and Volunteer Teachers from across the globe. With standardized lesson plans and teaching aids for a consistent learning experience, the children from government schools receive high quality education for a promising & rewarding future.

Here's What Other Volunteers Have to Say
Shweta Angne Volunteer Teacher at Nandarkhe School, Maharashtra
“Teaching these smart children from remote Indian villages gives you a real perspective of what a different world we live in. They are still new to the concept of a computer, internet and the technology boom outside their world but their curiosity is what leaves you enchanted. I started teaching at the Nandarkhe school that started this year in Maharashtra and I was excited as I got to teach in my mother tongue. It's been a privilege to teach the children of Nandarkhe in Maharashtra.”
Seema Mantry, Volunteer Teacher Uttarakhand
“eVidylaloka has given me the opportunity to connect with students who are eager to learn new concepts each day. Learn - Connect - Grow is the motto that eVidyaloka has taught me and I aspire to teach the same to my students.”
Sharmila Roy, Volunteer Teacher, Ghagarjori, Purulia, West Bengal
“Before starting the class, I was a bit doubtful if the students after a long gap of study in school, would find it difficult to cope with and that too through this new mode of learning through a smartphone. But I am very happy to see the eagerness and responsibility sense of the children. They are enjoying the class and very interactive.”
Abantika Ghosh, Volunteer Teacher, Hensla Junior High School
“I have taught several batches of students at Jabarrah High School but I was still worried about how the students of the new school, Hensla Junior would be. To my surprise, they were very welcoming and attentive. What I loved the most about them was that they were inquisitive. They started asking me questions on the first day itself. I am taking classes as scheduled i.e. twice a week for 40-60 mins. They are attending the classes well. I am looking forward to more classes with them.”
