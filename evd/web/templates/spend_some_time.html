eVidyaloka 's mission is to connect passionate volunteers to provide quality education for rural children
for
A knowledgeable & empowered Rural India

Today we have good enrollment and infrastructure in the Govt. schools. And the need of the hour is to impart quality in teaching. The lack is due to shortage of teachers and quality in available teachers

The supply of quality teaching may not exist where it is required, but they are spread across the globe. The role of eVidyaloka is to play the bridge in bringing them together on a common platform by connecting and empowering people and applying technology and help the children to reap the intended benefits.

This Academic Year 2015-16 begins on June 15th - We are reaching out to 50 Centers covering 2500+ Children in villages across Andhrapradesh, Telangana, Jharkhand, Karnataka and Tamil Nadu. This calls for over 400+ Teaching volunteers, apart from other volunteering contributions.

Minimum Pre-requisite:

        - 2 hours per week (any two 1-hr slots, Mon-Sat)
        - Minimum commitment for 6-12 weeks
        - Read & Write one local language (Hindi, Telugu, Kannada, Tamil)
Why we need Volunteers:

eVidyaloka believes in involving and empowering as many people as possible in this journey of enabling quality in education, from wherever they are. All of the results, ranging from Teaching, content dev, IT, Admin, so far are an outcome of volunteer contributions.

Frequently Asked Questions
►    I can read and write my native language, but have done my education in English medium and not conversant with the technical subject terms. Won't that be a problem?
Ans: Passion and commitment along with linguistic ability to read and write, we have observed most of the teachers in the past were able to manage quite comfortably to teach in local language. To support, both English version and the local language version books are available side by side for preparation. Infact, by 3rd or 4th class, the children start teaching you some of the language terms ☺

►    Can I teach only during weekends on Saturday and Sunday?
Ans: eVidyaloka classes happen within the school premises, during school hours. Hence we require volunteers to provide at least 1 hour in the weekday between 10 am to 4 pm (for JH & TN ) and 9am to 4pm (for AP). The second 1 hour could be on a Saturday and shall be worked out during the class planning discussion with the Class Administrators

►    I'm a working professional. How can I give teach during this time ?
Ans: More 60% of current teaching volunteers work full time in their professions. They adopt typicall any of these 3 approaches: 1) Take take the earliest slot at 9am or 10 am and head to work on these two days. 2) They check with their organisation/supervisor to take these two one hour slots for volunteering. And typically no one is being refused, provided the networking infrastructure supports 3) They manage one of the slots around lunch time and another one on a Saturday.
