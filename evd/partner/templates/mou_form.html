Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is MoU?

    • MOU i.e. Memorandum of Understanding is an agreement made when two or more parties outlining the rights and obligations of the parties to the agreement are into initial discussions. It is just a means for two parties to reach a decision.

  • Is MOU legally binding?

    • MOU does not constitute a legally enforceable obligation. It is commonly used for a non-binding contract. If an MoU has been drawn for consideration, like for exchange of money, etc., the document would become binding on the parties, else it is a non-binding contract. Clauses such as jurisdiction clause, applicable law, indemnification have binding effects to the agreement.

  • Is Stamp Duty on MOU payable?

    • Normally, no stamp duty is payable on MOU. However, if the MOU incorporates an agreement to purchase immovable property worth more than Rs. 100/- and if you need to produce it in the court, it should be stamped. A stamp duty paid document gets evidentiary value and is admitted as evidence in court. Document not properly stamped, is not admitted as evidence by the Court.

  • Who can use a Memorandum of Understanding?

    • Books of accounts comprise of a Journal, a Ledger book, a Trial Balance, Original and carbon copies of bills/invoices/receipts /, Cash Book, Profit and Loss A/c, Balance Sheet and Cash Flow Statements.

    • A Government Agency can execute an MOU with another agency within the same Government

  • What is Terms of Engagement?

    • Partners are required to provide terms of engagement for all to who they have a direct business relationship with, and therefore are paid directly by that Partner. This is a formal agreement between them, detailing the responsibilities of each party and the nature of the work required.

  • What are the contents in ToE?

    • Program Scope
    • Role and responsibilities
    • Infrastructure
    • Operations
    • Financials
    • Confidentiality

  • Should ToE be printed?

    • It is the reference for the Partner, It has to be read and agreed by the partners. But not necessary to be printed.

  • What should be the start date of MoU?

    • The month from when eVidyaloka/Online classes will be implemented in the identified school. The first date of the Month should be taken as start date of MoU.

  • How many schools can be added in one MoU?

    • The schools in which you wish to start the digital classroom program in the same month can be in one MoU.

  • If I want to initiate the digital classroom program in the schools in different phases, can I add the schools in same MoU?

    • The schools in which you planned to start the program in same month, can be added in MoU
    • The schools in which you will start the program in subsequent month, it should be in added in separate MoU with corresponding start date.

  • Why is the training cost included?

  • What does training offer?

    • It will be 2 days training to coordinator and Administrator and it includes Introduction to evidyaloka, roles and responsibilities, basic computer usage , using evidyaloka platform for managing the classes

  • How is the estimated Cost Calculated?

    • Estimated value is calculated based on the number of volunteer teachers requested for the program and the number of trainings for the schools onboarded. The unit pricing for one offering is 1000 INR per month and the training fees is 3000 INR for each coordinator.

Exhibit 2 - Terms of Engagement

General Information

Purpose:

         eVean Foundation is committed to providing quality education to the children in the rural villages of our motherland.

       This exhibit contains details of the operational arrangements eVean Foundation makes with {{mou_form.partner_org_name}} (hereafter referred to PARTNER) as by way of a partnership to achieve this goal - to provide quality education to the children in the rural schools.

Program Scope

eVidyaloka targets to extend educational services:

In Maths, Science and English

For Class V to VIII based on local State Board curriculum

Through set up of Digital Classroom & web conference online teaching

With the support of teacher volunteers and partnering organizations

Roles & Responsibilities:

PARTNER will,

  • 1. ensure that the PARTNER’s services are aligned with the Schools’ needs and objectives of the program
  • 2. ensure that it meets all the regulatory requirements and all its licenses/certifications etc. for carrying out the activities and are valid and current
  • 3. provide access to eVean Foundation’s and/or their authorized representatives to the locations or centres where PARTNER is conducting its activities, for the effective functioning of the program
  • 4. ensure that their agreements with contractors, school authorities or any third party working along with the PARTNER contains proper anti-corruption and confidentiality/data protection provisions (specifically complying with FCRA laws) and the responsibility in ensuring compliance with applicable laws of the land.

eVean Foundation has a commitment to comply with a policy on Data Protection and Privacy. Therefore it is required that PARTNER appreciates the sensitivity on all data on eVidyaloka including eVidyaloka staff, Volunteers, Donors, Schools and Students. PARTNER agrees to comply with Data Protection and Privacy Policy as given in Exhibit 3.


eVidyaloka Education for All Network Foundation will,

  • 1. Ensure that all eVean Foundation personnel comply with policy and systems in place at Schools, as shared by the PARTNER.
  • 2. Provide required data and information on the program to PARTNER/Schools participating in the program.
  • 3. Will maintain strict confidentiality of all information about Schools/students received under this MOU.
  • 4. Train the identified Class Coordinator(s), Partner coordinator(s).
  • 5. Maintain the technology platform and provide the necessary support needed for the remote class delivery operations
  • 6. Identify volunteer teachers for selected class levels and subjects, as mutually agreed.

Infrastructure

Digital Classroom setup

A typical eVidyaloka digital classroom consists of the following. However, the setup can vary and be different, which would still ensure, enablement of online classes.

  1. 1. Computer (CPU)
  2. 2. LED TV
  3. 3. Webcam (with in-built Microphone)
  4. 4. Cables to connect
  5. 5. Internet Dongle
  6. 6. Electrical utilities and Inverter systems
  7. 7. A soft board
  8. 8. Speaker Assembly

PARTNER will identify the students (school/academic class level/subject) and work with the concerned school administration to ensure the availability of classroom, the essential furniture for the classroom, and infrastructure as above.

PARTNER will work with concerned school administration for maintenance and safe upkeep of the classroom and will ensure that the assets are in working condition.

PARTNER will inform eVean Foundation for disruptions owing to any reason.

PARTNER will be responsible for the availability of the Internet through wired Broadband or Wi-Fi during the class hours.

PARTNER will ensure Power & Backup as necessary for the conduct of classes.


Operations

Training

eVean Foundation’s Academy team will coordinate and deliver the identified and agreed training and development requirements of the engagement, as per the agreed scope and budget.

Jupiter Platform Support

Setup and Maintenance of the platform: Jupiter will be hosted in cloud servers and will be maintained by eVidyaloka. eVidyaloka will also ensure system uptime and resolve issues that would be encountered, from time to time.

Any additional capabilities/features/feedback requested/provided will be taken up separately and considered for implementation as per the platform roadmap and discretion of eVean Foundation.

Volunteer Support

eVidyaloka will onboard and work with the volunteers mobilized by the PARTNER. Volunteer allocation will be taken up as per scope and budget after the volunteers have registered online with eVidyaloka and have been recommended to teach by eVidyaloka.

Dispute Resolution/Arbitration:

All disputes and differences arising out of or in connection with this MOU, including any question regarding its existence, validity or termination, shall be referred to and finally resolved by arbitration in Bangalore in accordance with the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 for the time being in force at the commencement of the arbitration, which rules are deemed to be incorporated by reference in this clause.

The Tribunal shall consist of three (3) arbitrators, two arbitrators nominated by each of the party and the two arbitrators, in turn, appointing the presiding arbitrator.

The award of the Arbitrator shall be final, conclusive and binding on both the parties to this MOU.

Force Majeure

This agreement is subject to Force Majeure clause. Neither party is deemed to have violated the contractual obligation if impacted by the Force Majeure events such as Acts of God, Acts of War, Acts of Government and local bodies, blockades, strikes, civil commotion, riots and the like. The party affected by Force Majeure shall give notice within a period of 15 days from the date of occurrence of Force Majeure indicating its cause and the period for which it is likely to last or affect execution.

FINANCIALS

Budget/Planning

All anticipated expenses and therefore the budget must be documented in a Scope & Budget Plan and executed by the parties.

CONFIDENTIALITY:

PARTNER shall regard and preserve as confidential all information that may be obtained from any source or may be developed in the course of this cooperation, by any means, during the period of the program or otherwise and is related either to the digital classroom activities of eVidyaloka, its clients, consultants or other entities with whom eVidyaloka does CSR activities; information relating to its employees; or such other information that may be considered confidential by its very nature
